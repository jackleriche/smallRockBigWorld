Venice

Transport

We arrived in Lyon after our ski at around 10pm, starving and everywhere was shut, Kat was getting HANGRY!! luckily we found a local delecasy restuarant that was Burger King in the shopping mall

After a 3 hour wait we boarded onto Flixbus at 1:15am for our 11h 30m coach journey to Venice, getting on too the bus it was completely full and stifflingly warm, we were unfortunate to not be able to sit with one another but after a few stops and into the Italian side of the Alps more seats became available. This bus although busier was actually more comfortable then the first, and while Kat didnt get much sleep, Jack on the other hand managed around 6 hours

While we were approaching Venice at 12:00 it was almost like driving to Atlantis, neither of us quite realised how low to the water the city is. Once we arrived at the bus stop is was about a 30 min walk to our Hostel

The City

Venice is a truely beautiful city, you can see why it is called the city of love. It is also however very easy to get truely and utterly LOST in all the tiny alleyways, we had to refer to Google maps on multiple occasions to get around effeciently

There is a lot of things to do in Venice, from visiting cathedrals, to lookking in Leonardo de Vinci museums. We were actually fortunate enough to arrive at the beginning of the largest carnival venice has to offer, which runs for an impressive 3 weeks from the 11th of Feb all the way to the beginning of Lent, this Carnival brings in alot of weird and wonderful sites, such as mascerade masks worn by the public and tourists, a ball happens everyday which brings out people dressed in 1600's ball attire. Unfortunately we were there for only the start but something happens every day and the events list

We spent alot of our time in Venice exporing the lanes and entering the free and paid attractions to get a feel for the history of the City
